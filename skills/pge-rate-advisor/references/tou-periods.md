# TOU Period Definitions — PG&E Residential Plans

Source: PG&E tariff data, effective March 1, 2026

---

## E-TOU-C (Peak Pricing 4–9 p.m. Every Day)
- **Summer** (June–September):
  - Peak: 4:00 pm – 9:00 pm, All Days
  - Off-Peak: All other hours
- **Winter** (October–May):
  - Peak: 4:00 pm – 9:00 pm, All Days
  - Off-Peak: All other hours

## E-TOU-D (Peak Pricing 5–8 p.m. Non-Holiday Weekdays)
- **Summer** (June–September):
  - Peak: 5:00 pm – 8:00 pm, Weekdays (non-holiday)
  - Off-Peak: All other hours (including all day weekends & holidays)
- **Winter** (October–May):
  - Peak: 5:00 pm – 8:00 pm, Weekdays (non-holiday)
  - Off-Peak: All other hours

*Note: E-TOU-D has narrower peak window (3 hrs vs 5 hrs) — beneficial for users who can shift most but not all evening usage.*

---

## E-ELEC (Electric Home — requires EV, battery, or heat pump)
- **Summer** (June–September):
  - Partial-Peak: 3:00 pm – 4:00 pm & 9:00 pm – 12:00 am, All Days
  - Peak: 4:00 pm – 9:00 pm, All Days
  - Off-Peak: 12:00 am – 3:00 pm, All Days
- **Winter** (October–May):
  - Partial-Peak: 3:00 pm – 4:00 pm & 9:00 pm – 12:00 am, All Days
  - Peak: 4:00 pm – 9:00 pm, All Days
  - Off-Peak: 12:00 am – 3:00 pm, All Days

**Rates (Summer):** Peak $0.55214 | Partial-Peak $0.39026 | Off-Peak $0.33358  
**Rates (Winter):** Peak $0.32063 | Partial-Peak $0.29854 | Off-Peak $0.28468

*E-ELEC has very low winter rates — excellent for homes with electric heating. No baseline credit.*

---

## EV2-A (Home Charging — requires EV)
- **Summer** (June–September):
  - Partial-Peak: 3:00 pm – 4:00 pm, All Days
  - Peak: 4:00 pm – 9:00 pm, All Days
  - Off-Peak: 9:00 pm – 3:00 pm next day
- **Winter** (October–May):
  - Partial-Peak: 3:00 pm – 4:00 pm, All Days
  - Peak: 4:00 pm – 9:00 pm, All Days
  - Off-Peak: All other hours

**Rates (Summer):** Peak $0.53809 | Partial-Peak $0.42760 | Off-Peak $0.22558  
**Rates (Winter):** Peak $0.41099 | Partial-Peak $0.39428 | Off-Peak $0.22558

*EV2-A off-peak rate is very low (~22¢) — best for EV charging and other shiftable loads overnight.*

---

## EV-B (Electric Vehicle Rate Plan B — requires EV)
Note: EV-B uses May–Oct / Nov–Apr season split (different from others)

- **Summer** (May–October):
  - Off-Peak: 11:00 pm – 7:00 am
  - Part-Peak: 7:00 am – 2:00 pm & 9:00 pm – 11:00 pm
  - Peak: 2:00 pm – 9:00 pm, Weekdays (non-holiday)
  - Off-Peak applies all hours on weekends & holidays
- **Winter** (November–April):
  - Off-Peak: 11:00 pm – 7:00 am
  - Part-Peak: 7:00 am – 2:00 pm & 9:00 pm – 11:00 pm
  - Peak: 2:00 pm – 9:00 pm, Weekdays (non-holiday)

**Rates (Summer):** Peak $0.62131 | Part-Peak $0.37720 | Off-Peak $0.26465  
**Rates (Winter):** Peak $0.43878 | Part-Peak $0.30677 | Off-Peak $0.23504

*EV-B has NO Base Services Charge (meter charge $0.04928/day instead). Best for very high EV users who charge overnight and can avoid peak completely.*

---

## EM-TOU (Master-Metered / multi-unit — for reference)
- Same peak windows as E-TOU-C (4–9pm all days)
- Has minimum bill (delivery minimum) instead of Base Services Charge
- Rarely applicable to single-unit residential — skip unless user is in multi-unit building without individual metering
